The key observation: the observed correlation is geometrically the cosine between two projection vectors — not a measure of the underlying relationship. When the two variables are projected through different, unknown measurement processes, this cosine can be positive, negative, or zero regardless of the true relationship. Replication failures across psychology, economics, and biomedicine may reflect this structural geometric fact — not failures of experimental rigor alone.
